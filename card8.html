VIII

The Avatar

In digital spaces, I create different versions of myself. Whether through profiles, avatars, or the curated parts of my personality that I choose to show, The Avatar represents the self I create online. It’s a reflection of how my data builds an image of me, one that’s not always the full picture but is still a version of who I am.

Upright Meaning

Creation: You have the power to shape and create your own identity, both online and in the real world. You can be whoever you choose to be, and you are in control of your self-image.

Duality: You are balancing multiple aspects of your life or identity. You have mastered the art of living in different worlds (digital, personal, professional).

Agency: You have a strong sense of agency over your life. You are creating the reality you want to live in.

Reversed Meaning

Fragmentation: Your identity is fractured, with too many personas pulling you in different directions. You may feel like you’re losing a sense of who you really are.

Disconnection: You may be overly invested in your virtual self or online identity, causing a disconnection from your real-world relationships or personal authenticity.

Illusion: Be careful not to create an illusion or false identity that doesn’t align with your true self. There is a risk of losing touch with reality.
